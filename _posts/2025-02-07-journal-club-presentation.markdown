As part of the [NELI Group Journal Club](https://uol.de/en/psychology/neurophysiology/neli-journal-club) 
at the University of Oldenburg, I presented the following paper:

**Zelano et al. (2016)** — *Nasal respiration entrains human limbic oscillations and modulates cognitive function*

This study shows that the rhythm of nasal breathing directly entrains neural 
oscillations in the hippocampus and amygdala, and that this entrainment has measurable 
consequences for memory and emotional processing. Notably, the effects are specific to 
nasal breathing — oral breathing does not produce the same coupling. The findings offer 
a neural basis for the cognitive benefits often attributed to breath-focused practices 
such as meditation.
